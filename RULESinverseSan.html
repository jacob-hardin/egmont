Inverse Sanity in the Dreamlands

Call of Cthulhu Optional Rule Variant

In the Dreamlands, madness is power. Rational thought becomes a liability, while fractured minds thrive in this ever-shifting world. This optional rule flips the Sanity system on its head: the lower your Sanity in the waking world, the more attuned you are in the dream realm.

Dream Sanity (Dream SAN)

Calculate your Dream SAN by inverting your current Sanity score:

Dream SAN = 99 – Waking SAN

Waking SAN Dream SAN Effect
90+ 9 or less Trapped in realism, unable to adapt
70–89 10–29 Disbelief clouds your judgment
50–69 30–49 Balanced, semi-capable of adapting
30–49 50–69 Comfortable in dream logic
10–29 70–89 Flexible, imaginative, able to reshape dreamscapes
0–9 90–99 Practically a dream-god, fully attuned to dream logic

Dream Skills

While in the Dreamlands, the following skills may be used or introduced. Adjust based on your campaign:

  • Lucid Shaping (POW) – Bend the dream to your will
  • Dreamwalking (INT) – Navigate bizarre landscapes
  • Gnosis (EDU or POW) – Understand and speak to dream entities

Optional Rules

  • Failing a SAN roll in the real world may increase Dream SAN, making the character more capable in dreams.
  • Trying to apply real-world logic in the Dreamlands (e.g., arguing gravity or math) may cause stress or dream damage.
  • Characters with high Dream SAN can reshape terrain, fly, summon dream-beings, or walk through solid objects—at the Keeper’s discretion.

Roleplay Tips

  • High Dream SAN characters are imaginative, detached, and perhaps godlike in confidence.
  • Low Dream SAN characters resist dream logic and suffer confusion, fear, or freezing under pressure.
  • Returning to the waking world may trigger trauma or inverse withdrawal.

This handout is a variant system and is not official Chaosium content. Modify freely to fit your campaign.
