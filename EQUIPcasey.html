Gear Checklist

Casey Searle

  • Soft-Sided Travel Backpack – Worn canvas, with patches from different cities.
  • Carhartt Jacket – Faded, oil-stained, and full of stories.
  • Cheap but Reliable Lighter – Always close; always borrowed.
  • Cigarettes – One carton at a time; always planning to quit next week.
  • Basic Foul Weather Gear
    • Rubber bib overalls (hand-me-down)
    • Yellow rain slicker
    • Fishing gloves with cut fingers
  • Steel-Toe Boots – Covered in salt crust and deck grime.
  • Faded Hoodies – Smells faintly of tobacco and engine grease.
  • Thermal Long Johns – Always worn under his jeans, even in spring.
  • Portable Hard Drive – Full of pirated Chinese films, family photos, and strange PDFs.
  • Rectangular Glasses – Slightly bent at the arms. Spare pair taped at the bridge.
  • Notebook – For sketches, Mandarin practice, and half-started stories.
  • Roll of Duct Tape – For everything: boots, backpack straps, emotional support.
  • Worn Wallet – Contains faded photo booth strip of his kids in Beijing.
  • USB Thumb Drive – Contains an old resume, music, and a letter he never sent.
  • Two Paperback Books
    • Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance
    • Three-Body Problem – In English, with margin notes in Mandarin
  • Phone & Battery Pack – Data is spotty, but he checks for messages anyway.
  • Travel Mug – With Chinese characters worn almost to invisibility.
  • Sleep Kit
    • Earplugs
    • Eye mask
    • Ratty sleeping bag liner
  • Pocket Mandarin Dictionary – Old, dog-eared, sentimental more than practical.
  • Stash of Instant Noodles – Mostly Chinese brands, comfort food for rough nights.
